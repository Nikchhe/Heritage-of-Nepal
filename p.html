About Patan Durbar Square

Patan Durbar Square, located in the heart of Lalitpur (also known as Patan), is one of the most magnificent architectural and cultural sites in Nepal. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, the square served as the royal palace complex of the Malla kings who ruled over Patan

History

It is a stunning display of Newar artistry, with intricately carved wooden windows, metal sculptures, and stone temples that reflect the city’s rich cultural heritage. The central attractions include the Krishna Mandir, built entirely of stone in the 17th century, the Bhimsen Temple, and the Hiranya Varna Mahavihar (Golden Temple). The Patan Museum within the square showcases a remarkable collection of traditional art and religious artifacts, offering deep insight into Nepal’s Hindu and Buddhist traditions.

Visit Us

Taxis and ride-sharing services are the most convenient options, taking around 20 to 30 minutes depending on traffic, while local buses from Ratnapark or Lagankhel provide a cheaper alternative. Upon arrival, visitors are required to pay a small entrance fee, which helps preserve the historic square and its monuments. The best time to visit is between October and April when the weather is clear and pleasant, making it ideal for sightseeing and photography.
