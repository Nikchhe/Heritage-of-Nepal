About Bhaktapur Durbar Square

Bhaktapur Durbar Square, located in the ancient city of Bhaktapur about 13 kilometers east of Kathmandu, is one of Nepalâ€™s most beautiful and well-preserved historical sites. Once the royal palace courtyard of the Malla kings, it stands as a masterpiece of medieval art, architecture, and culture.

History

he square became the royal palace complex and the political, religious, and cultural center of the Bhaktapur Kingdom. Under the rule of King Yaksha Malla in the 15th century, the city reached the height of its power and artistic achievement, with the construction of grand temples, palaces, and courtyards that showcased the extraordinary skill of Newar craftsmen.
